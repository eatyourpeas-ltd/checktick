# Email Authentication & Anti-Spoofing Policy

**Owner:** Dr Simon Chapman (CTO)
**Last Reviewed:** March 2026
**Scope:** checktick.uk and eatyourpeas.co.uk

This policy protects the integrity of email communications from EatYourPeas
Ltd and the {{ platform_name }} platform, preventing spoofing and phishing
attacks against NHS Trust partners and clinical users.

---

## 1. Outbound Email Authentication

The following DNS-based authentication records are maintained for all
domains from which EatYourPeas Ltd or {{ platform_name }} sends email.

### 1.1 Sender Policy Framework (SPF)

* **Status:** Active on checktick.uk and eatyourpeas.co.uk
* **Configuration:** SPF records include all authorised sending services
  (MailGun). All other
  senders are explicitly rejected.
* **Policy:** `-all` (Hard Fail) — any email from an unauthorised sender
  claiming to be from either domain is rejected outright.

### 1.2 DomainKeys Identified Mail (DKIM)

* **Status:** Active on checktick.uk and eatyourpeas.co.uk
* **Implementation:** 2048-bit RSA keys generated by the authorised
  sending services and published in DNS.
* **Rotation:** Keys are rotated immediately if a provider is changed
  or a compromise is suspected. Rotation is documented in the
  Infrastructure Technical Change Log.

### 1.3 DMARC

* **Status:** Active and enforced on checktick.uk and eatyourpeas.co.uk
* **Policy:** `v=DMARC1; p=quarantine;` — unauthenticated emails
  claiming to be from either domain are directed to the recipient's
  spam folder, protecting NHS Trust partners and clinical users from
  phishing using our domain identity.
* **Reporting:** Aggregate DMARC reports (RUA) are sent to the CTO
  for quarterly review to monitor for spoofing attempts and
  authentication failures.

---

## 2. Maintenance and Review

* **Quarterly Review:** The CTO verifies during spot checks that no
  unauthorised services have been added to the SPF record and that
  DKIM keys are current and valid.
* **Decommissioning:** When a third-party sending service is no longer
  in use, its SPF and DKIM entries are removed immediately. This is
  logged in the Infrastructure Technical Change Log.
* **DMARC Monitoring:** Aggregate reports are reviewed quarterly to
  identify any spoofing campaigns targeting either domain.

---

## 3. Inbound Email Security

### 3.1 Spam and Malware Filtering

Staff email accounts are provided through a business email platform
with built-in advanced threat protection including spam filtering,
phishing detection, malware scanning of attachments, and URL checking
against real-time blocklists including Google Safe Browsing and
Microsoft SmartScreen.

### 3.2 Inbound DMARC Enforcement

Inbound email is subject to SPF, DKIM, and DMARC validation. For
incoming emails from external senders, the mail platform follows
the sender's published DMARC policy — emails failing authentication
from domains with a quarantine or reject policy are handled
accordingly. This protects staff from spoofed emails impersonating
known partners including NHS Trusts.

### 3.3 Staff Awareness

Both staff members are trained in phishing identification as part of
NHS Data Security Awareness training. Suspected phishing emails are
reported to the CTO immediately per our Phishing Escalation and
Management Procedure.

### 3.4 Quarterly Review

The CTO reviews the spam and phishing dashboard of the business email
platform quarterly to identify targeting campaigns and verify that
filtering rules remain effective.
